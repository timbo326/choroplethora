Design Process and Design Thinking

Initial Design:

We conducted a brainstorming session to kick off the project 2. We mainly discussed the overall structure of the visualization after going through the data and project explanation. Figure 1 is a rough sketch which shows the basic idea of the visualization: A big choropleth map with level of state and county; when select states or counties, the dashboard aside displays many charts which reflect 13 variables; below the big choropleth map are small-multiples. Figure 2 is a more detailed sketch of our basic idea.

Considering to show 13 charts in one dashboard simultaneously will be so overwhelming, we analyzed the attribute of the variables and the relationship among the variables. We finally came up with a variable structure as figure 3 shows.

Picture 3

With the structure above, we design a pop-out dashboard layer over the big choropleth map which can show five different groups of charts. In doing so, enough space is available for legible small charts.The big choropleth map can get enough space to show counties level as well. Following (figure 4 - figure 9) is the visualization of our revised design including the big choropleth and detailed charts. Figure 4 shows the main page of the visualization. The button “show detail” controls the dashboard. Figure 5 to Figure 9 are the dashboard which displays the detailed charts. The bar on the top of the dashboard shows five groups: Total population within the locality, Median age by sex, Median household income & Per capita income, Income to poverty-level ratio, and Travel time to work. Under each option, two or three variables are visualized with appropriate charts. Charts groups can be switched by clicking the options on the top bar. The charts outside the dashboard represent the condition when two or more states/counties are selected (These charts don’t exact exist in the visualization ). In other word, if two or more locations are selected, the chart types are replaced by the chart types outside the dashboard.

Finally we decided to show the small multiples of Age distribution by sex (Figure 10), Poverty level by place of birth (Figure 11), Educational attainment by place of birth (Figure 12), Median household income & per capita income (Figure 13), Travel time to work (Figure 14), and Transportation to work (Figure 15).

Based on the variables, we made some assumptions and expect to find the hint from the small multiples. We have one hypothesis which is “Where people receive less education, where the poverty level higher”. We plan to compare Figure 11 with Figure 12 to looking for the evidence which might prove our idea. Besides, we will compare Figure 10 with Figure 13 to explore: Is there any relationship between median household income and per capita income? If so, what might be the cause? (Possible factors: Age distribution, education). Another question is: IF the transportation to work varies among different age groups? In order to find out a reasonable answer, we will look at Figure 10, 14, and 15 simultaneously. If we can get a plausible finding, the public transportation construction and traffic policy revision might benefit from the result.
